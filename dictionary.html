The language of Gen Z & Gen Alpha streetwear culture
DRIP
Your entire style, from head to toe. When someone has drip, their whole outfit and confidence is on point.
Fashion
FIRE
Something that's absolutely amazing. Used for music, fits, food, or anything that hits different.
Quality
SLAY
To absolutely dominate and do something with confidence and style. Often used when someone's outfit is perfect.
Attitude
BUSSIN
Originally for food that's really good, now used for anything that's excellent or hitting right.
General
CLEAN
A fit that's perfectly put together. Simple, stylish, no unnecessary extras - just pure style.
Style
VIBES
The energy or feeling something gives off. Good vibes = positive energy. Can describe music, places, or people.
Mood
PERIODT
Period with a T for emphasis. End of discussion - what you just said is facts and there's no argument.
Attitude
RIZZ
Charisma and charm, especially when talking to someone you're interested in. Short for "charisma."
Social
BET
Agreement or confirmation. Like saying "yes," "okay," or "for sure." Can also mean "you can count on it."
Agreement
NO CAP
No lie, telling the truth. "Cap" means lying, so "no cap" means you're being completely honest.
Truth

Language evolves. Culture creates. Style speaks.

Part of understanding streetwear is understanding the words that shape it.
