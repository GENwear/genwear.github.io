Bridging generational language gaps through fashion
DRIP
Originally from hip-hop culture, "drip" describes someone's complete style presentation - their clothing, accessories, and the confidence to wear them well. When someone "has drip," their fashion sense is considered exceptional.
Popularized in hip-hop, adopted by Gen Z and younger millennials
Fashion
FIRE
An expression of high praise meaning "excellent" or "amazing." Widely used by younger generations to describe anything impressive - music, clothing, food, or experiences. Often accompanied by fire emoji.
Originated in hip-hop, now mainstream across all young demographics
Quality
SLAY
To excel at something with confidence and style. Originally from drag culture, now used broadly when someone performs exceptionally well, especially in fashion or self-presentation. "You're slaying in that outfit" means you look incredible.
Drag culture → mainstream youth culture via social media
Attitude
BUSSIN
Originally used to describe food that tastes really good, now expanded to mean anything that's excellent or "hitting right." Popular among Gen Z and Gen Alpha, often used with enthusiasm about positive experiences.
African American Vernacular English (AAVE), spread via TikTok
General
CLEAN
Describes a look that's perfectly coordinated and well-executed. A "clean fit" means an outfit where everything works together harmoniously - no clashing colors, good proportions, and attention to detail. Implies understated excellence.
Evolved from "clean cut" in fashion terminology
Style
VIBES
The energy, mood, or feeling that something gives off. "Good vibes" means positive energy. Used to describe the atmosphere of places, the feeling of music, or the energy someone projects through their style and personality.
From "vibrations" in 1960s counterculture, modernized by millennials and Gen Z
Mood
PERIODT
"Period" with a "T" added for emphasis. Used to end a statement that the speaker considers final and unquestionable. Equivalent to saying "end of discussion" or "that's facts." Often used to emphasize strong opinions about style or fashion choices.
AAVE, popularized through social media and drag culture
Attitude
RIZZ
Short for "charisma." Refers to someone's charm and ability to attract others, especially in romantic contexts. Having "rizz" means you're smooth, confident, and good at conversation. Often associated with having style and confidence.
Coined by Twitch streamer Kai Cenat, popularized by Gen Alpha
Social
BET
An expression of agreement or confirmation, similar to saying "yes," "okay," or "sounds good." Can also express confidence, like "you can bet on it." Widely used across generations but particularly common in younger speech.
AAVE, adopted broadly across demographics since the 1990s
Agreement
NO CAP
"No lie" or "I'm telling the truth." "Cap" means lying or exaggerating, so "no cap" emphasizes honesty. Used when making a statement you want others to know is completely truthful, often about achievements or opinions.
Hip-hop culture, spread via social media platforms
